Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is an autoimmune disease where the body attacks the lining tissue of joints, causing chronic joint inflammation. While it primarily affects joints, it can also cause inflammation of organs, such as the lungs, eyes, skin, and heart. There is no cure for RA, but medications can stop the progression and ease symptoms.
